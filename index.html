About Me

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the Economics department at Rice University in Houston, Texas. My research interests include urban economics, transportation economics, and machine learning methods.

Prior to my Ph.D, I received a Bachelor of Arts from McGill University in Montreal, Quebec, and was a predoctoral researcher at Kellogg School of Management at Northwestern University in Evanston, Illinois.

Works in Progress

The Welfare Impacts of Subway Service Interruptions: Evidence from New York City

Job Market Paper

I estimate heterogeneous welfare impacts of unplanned subway service interruptions in New York City. Using a novel dataset of real-time train positions and posted service alerts, along with Census data on incomes and work locations, I estimate passengers' Value of Time in Transit in terms of wage income using a BLP-style estimator. In counterfactual exercises, I consider the short-term welfare improvements of targeted investment in new rolling stock and improved track infrastructure.

Estimation of Bayesian Spatial Learning Curves: An Application to New York City Taxi Drivers

I model new taxi drivers' learning processes in New York City using a Multi-Argmed Bandit framework on a spatial network. Drivers' search patterns change with market experience: they initially spend more time searching for passengers in outer boroughs in their first 100 shifts, but gradually move towards searching more in Manhattan over the next 100 shifts. These results lead to persistent unavailability of taxis in outerlying areas.

Too Hot to Handle? Urban Heat, Housing, and the Distribution of Welfare in Indian Cities

With Tanmay Devi

India is a rapidly urbanizing country which has seen substantial increases in heat exposure risk over the past decade. We model urban residents' responses to increased heat exposure through purchasing mitigation technologies (such as air conditioning) and migrating to cooler cities. We then close the model with production and housing sectors to estimate general-equilibrium effects on employment and housing expenditure.
